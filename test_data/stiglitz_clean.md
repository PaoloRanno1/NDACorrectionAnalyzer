In connection with your possible interest in a transaction involving Project Stiglitz (the "Company” and such potential transaction, the “Transaction”), the Discloser and/or related affiliates being directors or shareholders, will provide you with certain Confidential Information (as defined below). To assure the continued confidentiality of such Confidential Information, we request that you acknowledge and agree to the terms of this letter by signing and returning to us the duplicate copy of this letter.



Any reference in this letter to "Confidential Information" is to (i) the existence and contents of this letter and the existence and contents of the discussions or other communications between the Company, the Recipient and any third party interested to pursue a Transaction, about the Transaction, (ii) all information of whatever nature relating to the Transaction or the Company and its activities, which is made available to the Recipient or any of its Related Persons (as defined below) whether in writing, in disk or electronic form, orally or pursuant to site visits to premises and in any form or medium in which such information may be recorded or kept, and (iii) analyses, compilations, studies and other material prepared by the Recipient or its Related Persons which contain or otherwise reflect such information. Any reference to an "Associated Person" in this letter is in respect of a company, any person or entity, directly or indirectly, solely or jointly, controlling or controlled by that company. Any reference to a "Related Person" in this letter is in respect of a company, its Associated Persons, directors, employees, financial and other professional advisers, agents and consultants of that company at any time during the period in which the provisions of this letter apply.



Confidential Information does not include information which (i) is or becomes publicly available for any reason except a failure of the Recipient or any of its Related Persons to comply with the terms of this letter, or (ii) is or becomes available to the Recipient from a person other than the Company or its advisers, provided that such person is not prohibited to disclose such information by any contractual or other legal obligation to the Company or any other party, or (iii) is independently acquired or developed by the Recipient without breaching any of its obligations under this letter.



The Recipient undertakes to the Company that it will (i) keep the Confidential Information strictly confidential, (ii) ensure that the Confidential Information is protected in a secured place and with a reasonable degree of care, (iii) not use the Confidential Information for any other purpose than in connection with a Transaction or in any way detrimental to the Company, (iv) not disclose the Confidential Information to or discuss it with anyone other than any of its Related Persons and only to the extent necessary for a Transaction.



The Recipient shall ensure that each person to whom Confidential Information is disclosed is fully aware of the Recipient’s obligations under this letter and shall procure that each such person shall sign a copy of this letter for acknowledgement of and agreement to the terms hereof. The Recipient may not disclose Confidential Information to any other person than its Related Persons, unless the prior written consent of the Company is obtained in each instance. The Recipient shall keep a list of the persons to whom-to-whom Confidential Information is disclosed and shall make such list available to the Company on demand within a reasonable time.



In the event that the Recipient is required to disclose Confidential Information pursuant to any law or regulation, the rules of any listing authority or stock exchange or any order of any judicial, administrative or regulatory authority, it shall (in each case to the extent permitted by law and reasonably practicable) (i) inform the Company of the full circumstances of the disclosure and the information required to be disclosed, (ii) consult with the Company as to possible steps to prevent or limit disclosure and take such of those steps as the Company may reasonably require, (iii) gain assurances as to confidentiality from the body to whom the information is to be disclosed, (iv) exercise its best efforts to prevent disclosure of the identity of the Company, and (v) where the disclosure is by public announcement, agree the wording with the Company in advance.



If the Recipient is unable by reason of law to inform the Company before Confidential Information is disclosed under paragraph (5), the Recipient shall (to the extent permitted by law) inform the Company as soon as possible after the disclosure of the full circumstances of the disclosure and the information that has been disclosed.



The Recipient shall immediately inform the Company of the full circumstances of any disclosure upon becoming aware that Confidential Information has been disclosed in breach of the terms of this letter.



If the Recipient ceases to be interested in a Transaction, and in any event upon the written request of the Company, the Recipient will immediately at its own cost and expense (i) return to the Company (without keeping any copies) all documents containing Confidential Information or relating to the negotiations or discussions about the Confidential Information or a Transaction, whether or not in the possession of the Recipient; (ii) destroy (without keeping any copies) all reports, analyses, compilations, studies or other documents which contain, derive from or otherwise reflect any Confidential Information, whether or not in the possession of the Recipient; (iii) expunge all Confidential Information from any computer, word processor or other device containing the Confidential Information and belonging to the Recipient, its Related Persons or any other person by which it is held; and (iv) if so requested by the Company, the Recipient shall deliver to the Company a certificate signed by the Recipient confirming that the obligations contained in this paragraph have been complied with. The Recipient is permitted to retain a copy of Confidential Information if required by any applicable law, rule or regulation or by any competent judicial, governmental, supervisory or regulatory board.



For a period of two (2) years from the date of this letter, the Recipient and its Associated Persons shall agree not to directly or indirectly solicit for employment or employ any employees, directors or managers of the Company. Exclusions apply to hiring through a public general advertisement and employees, directors or managers who approach without any solicitation by the Recipient.



For a period of two (2) years from the date of this letter, the Recipient and its Associated Persons shall agree not to solicit any client directly or indirectly from the Company to whom the Company provides services and that has been disclosed in the Confidential Information. 



The Recipient shall direct all communications, Confidential Information requests and enquiries relating to the Confidential Information only to the Discloser or such other representative(s) of the Company as the Company may subsequently notify to the Recipient.



The Recipient acknowledges and agrees on behalf of itself and its Related Persons that the Confidential Information does not purport to be all inclusive and that no representation or warranty, express or implied, is made by the Company or any of its directors, officers, employees, agents or advisers as to the accuracy, reliability or completeness of the Confidential Information. The Recipient agrees, for itself and on behalf of its Related Persons that neither the Company nor any of its Related Persons shall have any liability to the Recipient or any of its Related Persons in respect of the Confidential Information disclosed to it or them. 



This letter does not constitute a formal offer or an intention to make an offer. The Company is entitled to reject any proposals and to terminate discussions at any time and for any reasons without any liability to the Recipient or any of its Related Persons.



The invalidity or unenforceability of any provision of this letter shall not affect the validity or enforceability of any other provision of this letter. Any such invalid or unenforceable provision shall be replaced or deemed to be replaced by a provision that is considered to be valid and enforceable and which interpretation shall be as close as possible to the intent of the invalid and unenforceable provision. 



The provisions of this letter shall remain in effect until the two (2) years from the date of this letter.
