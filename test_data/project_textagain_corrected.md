## INTRO

The goal of this Agreement is to facilitate the exchange of confidential
information between Strada Partners and Textgain (Parties), and in
consideration of and as a condition to the provision of such
information, the parties agree to keep such information confidential in
accordance with the following terms.

## PARTIES AGREE

1.  **[CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION]{.smallcaps}**

> **Confidential Information** is any information in any form in
> relation to the business, structure, systems, operations, employees,
> clients, potential clients, know-how, including trade secrets, of a
> party and any third party's information which is disclosed, and all
> other information that a reasonable person knows or reasonably should
> understand is confidential.

2.  **[OBLIGATIONS]{.smallcaps}**

    1.  The disclosing party will provide Confidential Information to
        > the receiving party for the purposes of the Project only. When
        > Confidential Information is provided, the receiving party
        > must:

<!-- -->

a.  prevent any disclosure of Confidential Information to third parties
    including, without limitation, other clients of parties, competitors
    of parties and the public, including the press,

b.  restrict circulation of the Confidential Information to personnel,
    auditors, legal and financial advisors++ [and affiliated
    companies]{.mark} ++that have a need-to-know in connection with the
    Project,

c.  not use the Confidential Information for any other purposes than the
    Project.

    2.  Each party will ensure that each of the entities or persons to
        > whom disclosure is permitted under article 2.1 will comply
        > with the obligations pursuant to this clause.

<!-- -->

3.  **EXCLUDED INFORMATION**

> The obligations of **article 2.1** do not apply to Confidential
> Information which:

a.  is or becomes public knowledge, other than through unauthorised
    disclosure by the receiving party,

b.  is known by the receiving party prior to the first occasion of
    access to such Confidential Information, it being understood that
    this exception will not apply to any Confidential Information which
    was prepared by or on behalf of parties for each other in connection
    with the Project,

c.  is developed independently by the receiving party without the
    benefit of access or reference to the Confidential Information.

<!-- -->

4.  **EXCEPTIONS**

> 4.1 The receiving party may disclose the Confidential Information
> where disclosure is required by law, by an irrevocable decision of a
> competent court or by a competent official supervisory authority
> including an authority supervising the disclosure of price sensitive
> information by listed institutions to investors.
>
> 4.2 The receiving party is entitled to disclose the Confidential
> Information to the extent such disclosure is reasonably necessary to
> comply with the receiving party's obligation to disclose price
> sensitive information to its investors considering the advice of a
> reputable law firm or the in-house legal department.

3.  In the event Confidential Information must be disclosed in
    > accordance with **article 4.1 or article 4.2**:

<!-- -->

a.  the receiving party must give the disclosing party notice of the
    disclosure as soon it is practicably possible to allow the
    disclosing party to prevent such disclosure,

b.  such disclosure must be covered by protective measures which
    reasonably protect the interest of the owner of the Confidential
    Information (if disclosed under **article 4.1**),

c.  the receiving party must anonymise such Confidential Information as
    far as possible,

d.  the receiving party must inform the relevant authority that the
    Confidential Information is subject to a duty of confidence by this
    agreement (if disclosed under **article 4.1**).

<!-- -->

5.  **UNAUTHORISED ACTS**

    1.  Each party must:

<!-- -->

a.  notify the other party promptly, and in any case within 48 hours, if
    it suspects that an unauthorised disclosure of the other party\'s
    Confidential Information, or an attempt thereof, has occurred,

b.  promptly furnish to the other party full details of the unauthorised
    disclosure, or attempt thereof,

c.  assist the other party in investigating any unauthorised disclosure,
    or attempt thereof, of Confidential Information,

d.  promptly take adequate remedial measures and use all reasonable
    efforts to prevent a recurrence of any such unauthorised disclosure
    or attempt thereof, of Confidential Information and the continued
    unauthorised possession, use or knowledge of such disclosed
    Confidential Information.

<!-- -->

6.  **PENALTY**

> 6.1 If one of the parties fails to comply with the provisions of this
> agreement it is in default by operation of law and it forfeits an
> immediately due and payable penalty of , ++[the amount of which will
> be established by the courts]{.mark},++ \--EURO 10,000 per
> incident,\-- notwithstanding the right of the other party to claim all
> actual costs and damages and to invoke any other rights and remedies
> it may have pursuant to this agreement and applicable law.
>
> 6.2 The amount of the penalty as set out in clause 6.1 will be
> ++[significantly]{.mark}++ reduced \-- to EURO 5,000 \--if the
> receiving party notifies the disclosing party in accordance with
> clause 5 before the disclosing party notifies the receiving party of
> the breach.

7.  **MISCELLANEOUS**

    1.  Neither this agreement, nor any disclosure of Confidential
        > Information under this agreement, in any way:

<!-- -->

a.  grants the parties any right or license under any intellectual
    property right now or hereafter owned or controlled by the other
    party,

b.  grants a party any right to use the other party\'s name, mark or
    logo outside its organisation in connection with the Project without
    the express prior written consent of the other party,

c.  commits the parties to perform any work, enter into any license,
    business engagement or other agreement,

d.  limits the parties from developing, manufacturing or marketing
    products or services which may be competitive with those of the
    other party,

e.  limits the parties from assigning or reassigning its employees in
    any way,

f.  creates any joint relationship or authorises the parties to act or
    speak on behalf of the other party,

    1.  limits the parties from entering into any business relationship
        with any other party.

<!-- -->

8.  **TERM AND SURVIVAL**

    1.  This agreement stays in full force and effect for a period of
        ++[2]{.mark}++ \--5\-- years after this agreement is signed by
        both parties.

    2.  Upon request by the disclosing party or upon 14 days after
        > termination of this agreement, the receiving party shall
        > promptly delete or, at the disclosing party's written request
        > return, all Confidential Information. The receiving party may
        > however retain such Confidential Information to the extent and
        > as long as (i) such retention is required by law or (ii) a
        > full and permanent deletion of the Confidential Information is
        > not possible due to technical reasons. The obligations of the
        > receiving party and the rights of the disclosing party under
        > this agreement will continue to apply in the event of the
        > aforementioned continued retention.

9.  **GOVERNING LAW**

    1.  This agreement and any dispute arising out of or in connection
        > with this agreement, will be construed in accordance with and
        > exclusively governed by the laws of Belgium, without reference
        > to conflicts of law principles. The parties hereby irrevocably
        > submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Antwerp,
        > Belgium, with respect to any dispute, suit or proceeding
        > arising out of or concerning this agreement.
